> **Abstract:** Detecting manipulated media in cross-domain scenarios remains a formidable challenge due to the domain shift vulnerability of existing methods. **GranIT** is a multi-granularity framework designed to robustly detect face forgeries by synergizing global spatial contexts, localized facial boundaries, and high-frequency micro-artifacts.

---

## Key Contributions

1. **Parameter-Efficient Spatial Backbone**: Utilizes a shared Vision Transformer (ViT) equipped with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA), significantly reducing trainable parameters while preserving the rich representations of the pre-trained LAION-2B weights.
2. **Learnable High-Pass Filter (LHPF) & Frequency-Guided AFC**: Autonomously localizes forgery-rich boundary regions while maintaining a strict 1:1 RGB-frequency spatial correspondence, overcoming the structural disruption of traditional frequency transforms like FFT.
3. **Cascaded Cross-Scale Interrogation Mechanism (CCSIM)**: A novel attention-based fusion module that explicitly anchors localized anomalies to the holistic image context, effectively mitigating negative transfer on heavily degraded and highly compressed datasets.

---

## Architecture Overview

The GranIT framework operates through three synergistic branches:
* **Global Branch**: Captures the holistic context of the wide input image.
* **Local Branch**: Focuses on specific forgery-rich boundary regions extracted by the Adaptive Facial Crop (AFC) module.
* **Micro Branch**: Extracts high-frequency synthesis artifacts using a dynamically constrained LHPF.
